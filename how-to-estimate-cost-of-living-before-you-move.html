How to estimate cost of living before you move

Cost of living research is easier when you compare the same budget categories every time. Start with rent, then add utilities, insurance, food, and a general miscellaneous allowance. This creates a rough monthly total you can compare across states or cities.

Rent is usually the biggest variable, but it should not be the only one you look at. Some states have lower housing costs but higher insurance or utility bills. Food spending also changes depending on local pricing and the kind of household you have.

A smart way to research is to start broad at the state level, then narrow down to metro areas after you have a shortlist. That saves time and gives you a quick sense of which locations are worth deeper investigation.

To compare broad monthly estimates quickly, use the state living cost calculator and review the total side by side before making a shortlist.
