How to calculate BMI step by step

Body Mass Index, or BMI, is one of the simplest ways to compare weight to height. It is not a perfect health measurement, but it is widely used because it is fast, familiar, and easy to calculate.

In metric units, the formula is weight in kilograms divided by height in meters squared. For example, if a person weighs 70 kg and is 1.70 meters tall, the BMI is 70 divided by 1.70 squared, which is about 24.2. In imperial units, the formula uses pounds and inches with a multiplier of 703.

After calculating the number, compare it with common categories. Below 18.5 is underweight, 18.5 to 24.9 is healthy, 25 to 29.9 is overweight, and 30 or above is obese. These categories are used for quick screening, not final diagnosis.
