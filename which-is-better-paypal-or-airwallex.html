What is PayPal? What is Airwallex?

PayPal launched in 1998, dominates consumer and SME online payments. It offers branded checkout, buyer protection, and vast reach – but international fees are famously opaque. According to PayPal’s official merchant fee schedule, cross-border commercial transaction fees range from 1.29% + fixed fee up to 4.5% + currency conversion markup of 3–4% above the base rate.

Airwallex is a modern fintech unicorn valued at $5.5B (Forbes, 2025), built for global business. It provides multi-currency accounts, low-cost FX (interbank rate + ~0.5%), and international payment rails. Airwallex powers businesses like Papaya Global and Qantas. Their pricing page boasts transparent international transfers: 0.2%–1% depending on volume, no hidden currency spread.
