Fraction simplification tricks for students

Simplifying fractions means rewriting them in the smallest equivalent form. The fastest method is to divide both the numerator and denominator by the same greatest common factor.

For example, 18/24 can be simplified because both numbers are divisible by 6. When you divide 18 and 24 by 6, the result becomes 3/4. The value is the same, but the fraction is easier to read and compare.

A good habit is to test small factors first: 2, 3, 5, and 10. If none of those work, look for larger common factors. Another trick is to check whether both numbers are even, because dividing by 2 repeatedly often simplifies a fraction quickly.
