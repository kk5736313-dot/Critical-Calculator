Understanding Your Results

Mortgage lenders typically use the 28% rule, which suggests that your total housing payment should not exceed 28% of your gross monthly income.

Example: Income needed for 300k mortgage

If you take a $300,000 mortgage at 6% for 30 years, your base payment is roughly $1,798. Adding taxes and insurance might bring it to $2,300/month. To afford this comfortably under the 28% rule, your gross annual income should be approximately $98,500.
