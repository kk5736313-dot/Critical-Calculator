2. Stripe – Best API & Developer Experience

Stripe remains the most developer-friendly platform, with SDKs in every language and seamless subscription logic. The standard rate for US cards is 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction. But startups quickly discover the hidden fees: +1.5% for international cards, +1% currency conversion, and +0.5-0.8% for Stripe Billing subscriptions. Effective rate for cross-border SaaS can exceed 5.5%.

Best for: Startups with a strong engineering team, US-only sales, and low international volume.

If you are a non-US startup, Stripe's 1.5% international surcharge and 1% conversion fee will add up quickly.

3. PayPal – High Recognition, High Cost

PayPal is accepted in over 200 markets and offers buyer protection, but the fees are punishing for growth. Domestic rates range from 2.99% to 3.49% + fixed fee. International payments add a 1.5% cross-border fee, making total ~4.4% + fixed fee. Then PayPal adds a 3–4% FX markup when converting currencies. A $10,000 international sale can cost ~$840 in fees.

Watch out: PayPal is known for freezing accounts for 180 days with no warning, a cash flow killer for startups.

Best for: Very early-stage startups that need immediate brand trust, but switch as you scale.

4. Wise Business – Low-Cost International Transfers

Wise (formerly TransferWise) offers multi-currency accounts with mid-market exchange rates and no FX markup. Sending money costs as low as 0.41% in ideal corridors. However, for business receivables, Wise charges a conversion fee of ~1.6–1.7% of the amount plus a $2 FIRA fee. All-in effective cost lands around 2% per transaction.

Unlike full-stack payment gateways, Wise does not offer card acquiring, subscription billing, or merchant of record. Great for paying contractors or holding balances, but not a replacement for a primary payment gateway.

Best for: Freelancers, agencies, and startups needing a cost-effective way to send cross-border payroll.
